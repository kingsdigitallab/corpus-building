Apparatus criticus

Text controlled against photographs and drawing of Salinas; The final I is not clearly visible in either published photograph, but is recorded in the drawing of Salinas; autopsy is required

translation

To Titus Fulvius (Fulvus) Aurelius Antoninus, son of Imperator Caesar Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus. Lucius Aponius Rufinus (set this up) to mark the honour of his holding of the sevirate, at his own expense.

commentary

Titus Aurelius Fulvus Antoninus was the short-lived twin brother of the emperor Commodus, son of the emperor M. Aurelius, born at Lanuvium on 31 August 161, and died in 165. His name often appears in the form Fulvius, as here.
