Apparatus criticus

Text after ILTermini with changes based on the autopsy 3: Bivona (1994) reads s(uo) at the end of line three. This is not evident on autopsy, but would complete the formula.

English translation

[...] To [...] Lesbius, freedman of Publius [..., and ...] Damophilus, freedman of [..., ?] made [this] from his own resources.

commentary

It is clear that lines 1-2 address the dedicatees, both freedmen, but that line 3 would have included the name of the dedicant. The cognomen Lesbius, of Greek origin, is common. The cognomen Damophilus, also of Greek origin, is less common but nonetheless elsewhere attested in the empire.
