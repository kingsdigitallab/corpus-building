Description
Three blocks of stone (type not specified), well finished, with moulding along the upper edge and inscription below; assumed by Manganaro and Orsi to join as part of a funerary monument. The first is described in report of 1841 as 5.5 palms wide, the second 4 palms, the third 4.5 palms. Messina proposes that the blocks are part of the facade of a nymphaeum uncovered in 1961.
