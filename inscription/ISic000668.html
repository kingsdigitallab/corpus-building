Apparatus criticus

Text from Manganaro 1963

English translation

[after the edition of Cebeillac-Gervasoni] The ambassadors who went to Rome and to Lanuvium, according to the instructions which they had received, Philarchos, son of Philarchos, Lamp[---, son of ---], and Xoarchos, son of Meniskos, report that they went to Lanuvium and that they have renewed links of hospitality and of kinship between the people of Centuripe and the people of Lanuvium, and that they bring a decree from the senate of the Lanuvians: Furius, son of Publius, the dictator, and Gaios Atellios son of [---] and [---] son of Gaios, the aediles, convened the senate [---] days before the Kalends of December. The decree was composed by Leukios son of [---], Markios son of Quintus, and Leukios Kaptios, son of Markos. Concerning those matters about which Philiarchos, son of Philiarchos, Lamp[-- son of ---] and Xoarchos, son of Meniskos, ambassadors of the Kentoripini [---?---] of the people and the senate of the Kentoripini [---?---], in order to renew the links of hospitality and of kinship and [---?---] for their descendants, as a memorial [---?---] just as the ambassadors, fine and noble men, have renewed [---?---] have received [---?---] the colony (apoikia) of the Kentoripini [---?---].
