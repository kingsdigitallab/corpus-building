commentary

It is not possible to expand the text of block A: Facella (2006: 178 n.127) suggests that an iota can be read before the epsilon, but this is only the line of breakage on the stone, and his suggestion to read the name Hieron (i.e. King Hieron II of Syracuse, c.269-215 BC), if the monumentalisation of the agora belongs in the second century BC, is unlikely (if tempting).
