Inscription

Layout
The text consists of two inscriptions, arranged on two different parts of the same object. The first (a) is on the underside of the foot: it consists of five letters arranged in one line and a fifth letter not aligned with the others. It has been highlighted with white painting in modern times to make it more legible. The second (b) is on the bottom of the interior of the cup and consists of three letters not aligned with each other.

commentary

The text appears to consist of a part (fragm.b), presumably previously scratched, in Greek and a part (fragm.a), later graffitied, in Elymian language. The Greek text can be interpreted as an Attic abbreviation, while the text in Elymian could consist of numerals pertaining to the culture of Segesta.
