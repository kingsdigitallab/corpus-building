Apparatus criticus

Manganaro 1989

translation

Here rest in peace the sons of Te[...] a scribe of the sacred palace, that is [...]. [The elder of the two] lived for [circa] ten years and three months; Stefanus lived for seven (?) years and [...] months. They were interred together in the month of January, on the [...th] indictio. But may nobody, at any point in time, order or desire the removal of this tomb along with these wholly blameless boys.

commentary

Reconstruction contains Manganaro's conjectures. M. tentatively reconstructs the name of the father as Terentiani (in genitive) in his illustration (Fig. 52a), but maintains Tel[...] in his textual reconstruction. The former, he concedes, 'Ã¨ solo una congettura'. M. restores the text as a tomb of a scribo sacri palatii (an imperial official linked to imperial administation and subsequently recruitment of troops in the provinces). Note also a warning against desecrating the tomb.
