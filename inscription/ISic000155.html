commentary

The surface of the stone makes the reading of line 1 less than transparent, but the S seems certain, and ELPI is unlikely to be anything other than the name (H)Elpis. Colax in line 2 likewise seems clear, even if a poorly attested name. Whether the stone is part of a funerary inscription or records the activities of two liberti (building, dedication, etc.), of whom the first appears to be female, cannot be clarified. Several of the reports of this text appear to unite the obverse with the reverse to produce a single funerary inscription, but the reading of the reverse in published editions is wrong (see ISic001876) and there is clearly no connection at all between the two texts.
