English translation

To Valerius Licinius Licinianus, most noble Caesar, the Res Publica of the people of Halaesa (set this up), vowed to his divine power and majesty

Italian translation

A Valerio Licinio Liciniano nobilissimo Cesare la Res Publica del popolo di Halaesa (dedica questa), devota al suo potere divino ed alla sua maestà

Description
A heavily damaged marble slab, of which 30 fragments survive, floated into a reconstruction on a panel. Most of the right side, much of the upper edge and parts of the left edge are preserved, but the lower edge is entirely lost and there are substantial lacunae throughout the text.

commentary

The abbreviation Res P(ublica) in line 5 is adopted as the form used on other examples from Halaesa (ISic003587 and ISic003590)

Scibona offers a clear rationale for assuming that this refers to Valerius Licinius Licinianus the young, son of the Augustus of 308-324, and himself Caesar between 317 and his death, aged 11, in 326 AD.

ISic000026 from Palermo is a dedication to Valerius Licinianus the elder, before 314 AD.
