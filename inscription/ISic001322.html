commentary

The epitaph presents the traditional structure with the name of the deceased, the indication of his age (with the verb ζάω in the aorist form) and a second sentence with the name of the dedicant in the nominative form and the dedication verb (ποιέω in the aorist form). The formulae and the palaeography are characteristic of Catania and of a 2nd-century CE dating, according to Korhonen (2004: 229; contra Manganaro 1959: 242, who dated the inscription to the 4th cent. CE). The epitaph was dedicated to a libertus Saboukis Kallimorphos by his patron Saboukis Zoilos (πάτρων is not part of the proper name of the dedicant, as Manganaro suggested: see J. and L. Robert, BE 1961: 268 nr. 861). The patron belonged to the gens Sabucia, who seems to have owned several latifundia in Sicily, as numerous toponyms attest: see Manganaro 1959.
