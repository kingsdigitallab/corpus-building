Description
A limestone tympanum from a small aedicola. The left and right ends of the tympanum have been sawn off at a later date (the block was re-used as a threshold in a mediaeval structure. The upper and lower cornice have been damaged. The inscription runs below the lower cornice. The framed pediment of the tympanum is empty.

commentary

Silvestrini 2014 plausibly suggests that this is a dedication set up by L. Plinius L.f. Rufus, legatus pro praetore of Sextus Pompeius and praetor designate for 33 BC, already attested at Lilybaeum in ILLRP 426 = ISic000007. The inscription is referenced within AE 2007.678-679, although neither lemma there concerns this text.
