Apparatus criticus

Text from autopsy; There is no immediately clear answer as to what the sign "Ↄ", a reverse C, symbolises here; cf. ISic000146, CIL 6.15139 (Rome) for analogous use, as pointed out by Bivona (1994). Understanding Ↄ as deriving from 'circiter' is one attractive interpretation.

English translation

To the Shades of the Underworld. Cornelia Scepsis; she lived [almost?] 38 years. Auctus erected this for his most dutiful wife.

commentary

The husband of the deceased, Auctus, is known only by his cognomen. It is probable that Auctus is the same individual as identified ISic000161 and ISic000164, all found at the same time and in the same location, from which we can discern his name: L. Cornelius Auctus. Corneliae are common in Thermae Himeraeae, although the cognomen Scepsis is unknown in Sicily
