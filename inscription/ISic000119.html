Text after Fazello and Gualtherus; Gualtherus 1624[1625]: ADVENIO
4: Fazello: CONIUGI

English translation

Helias (set this up) for her dutiful husband Lucius Acilius Adventus.

commentary

The reports in Fazello and Gualtherus make it highly likely that this stone was originally found in Termini (like the others attested in private houses in the 16th and 17th century in the town), but was subsequently transferred to a collector in Messana, where Gualtherus saw it. Bitto provides an extended discussion of the onomastics, but the assumption that it is from Messana is unjustified, and her attribution of the reading 'Adventus' to a correction of Mommsen is to ignore the original report in Fazello / misread Mommsen's apparatus. It seems preferable however to follow Gualtherus' report of the form 'coiugi' over Fazello's likely 'normalisation' of 'coniugi'.
