Apparatus criticus

  • The text of IG reproduces that of Carini. Both are correct in their representation of the letters on the stone, but omit the interpunct and ligature in line 3, the ligature in line 4, the diaeresis in line 5, and the staurogram at the end of the text; all these features are recorded by Orsi.

Lettering
Slightly irregular but neatly carved plain letters without serifs. Alpha has straight bar; Epsilon is rectilinear, with mostly equal length bars; Theta is large and voice, with a full width horizontal bar; Kappa generally has short arms; Lamda has a slight extension at the top; Mu (in ligature) has vertical virst and last, with diagonals extending only half-way down;' rho has small head, closed; sigma is lunate but rectilinear; omega is lunate. Interpuncts are used around numerals.
