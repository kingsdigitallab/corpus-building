Description
Plinth (support for a stele) with moulding around the base. The plinth is heavily damaged and all the upper portion is lost and much of the lower moulding on the sides also; the lower part of the inscribed face is preserved, marked by a number of deep striations in the surface. The plinth is the left one of a pair mounted on a single base, classified as an epitymbion of type C.

commentary

The majority of the epitaphs attested from this necropolis display a single name in the genitive. However, given the fragmentary state of this stone, it cannot be ruled out that this is the patronymic of the deceased, with a first name on the now lost upper portion of the stone. Against this, the second plinth from this funerary monument (ISic004420) clearly contains only a single name in the genitive, and it is therefore more likely that only the single name was also recorded here. The evidence of ISic004415/ISic004416 and of ISic004417/ISic004418 suggests that the two individuals commemorated by this stone and its pair ISic004420 were related (bothers?).
