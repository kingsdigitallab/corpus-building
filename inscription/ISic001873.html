Inscription

Layout
Text over five lines, neatly respecting gridlines to the base of each line and with marginal lines to left and right - that on the left is approximately followed, that on the right is ignored; an additional gridline below is unused. Some use of interpuncts and vacats in the text. The first line shows clear traces of red paint in the letters.
Text condition
No data
Lettering
Text is incised in fairly tall, thin, neat and plain letters mostly without serifs. Alpha has downward left sloping cross-bar; epsilon is lunate, with shorter and sometimes detached middle bar; zeta is straight; eta sometimes has detached cross-bar (line 4); kapa has short arms; mu has angled strokes, the mid-strokes only descending half-way, and no upward extension of the apexes; omicron is slightly smaller than other letters; pi does not overlap; rho i slcosed; sigma is lunate. Use of a simple/stylised hedera in lines 2 and 4, and letters are somewhat irregularly spaced especially in the final line.

commentary

The name Kopria is rare, with only a single Sicilian attestation, from Catania. There is no clear basis for determining the potential relationship between Kopria, Mousa and Gamike, except that it seems likely that Mousa was the child of one of the other two. See also ISic001872 and ISic001871.
