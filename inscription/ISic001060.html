English translation

When Aristion son of Agath[---] and Philistion son of Epikrates were gymnasiarchs, the neaniskoi Hieroneioi (dedicated this).

Italian translation

Quando Aristione figlio di Agath[---] e Filistione figlio di Epicrate erano ginnasiarchi, i neaniskoi Hieroneioi (dedicarono questo)

Physical description

Support

Description
Block of the local limestone, originally in situ above the entrance to the rock-cut structure of the gymnasium, but then cut from the site and removed. The epigraphic field is 212 x 64 cm. The face has two large round holes cut into the second line, and a further element cut out of the end of the first line (upper right corner). The lower edge is damaged, obscuring part of line 4.

Lettering
Very regular neat v-cut letters. The text is centred on the stone. Note the use of horizontal lines/dashes between the name and patronym of the officials. Sigma is four bar and open; Pi has short second vertical; omicron and omega are small, the latter closed.
