Description
A very wide text laid out across multiple marble slabs of differing widths, of which 8 pieces are preserved (originally 6, but two have become further fragmented over time). The slabs were most likely displayed on the facade of the building to which they relate.

Lettering

Letters are wide and omicron, phi, sigma, epsilon and theta are, on occasion formed by overlarge circles. Epsilon, sigma and omega are lunate. Xsi is formed in a distinctive, ornate fashion. Interpuncts are used occasionally, and formed of an 's' shape, sometimes forward, sometimes backward facing. Numerals are marked with a supraline.

commentary

The word ПҖО®ПғОұПӮ is a hapax. It is plausibly explained by Manganaro (1958-1959: 28 n.94) as being a calque in Greek of the Latin word pensum, signifying a weight of a fixed quantity.
