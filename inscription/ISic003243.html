commentary

Korhonen considers the epitaph from Catania because of its palaeographic characteristics, although its structure is remarkable: the name of the dedicant is in the nominative form and is followed by the name of the deceased in dative (with the specification of the degree of kinship) and by the dedication verb with the expression μνήμης χάριν; another sentence with the indication of the age follows (see in Catania also IG 14.476 = ISic001299). Strangely after the indication of the deceased there is a participle ζῶν referring to the dedicant, who made the epitaph while alive (see IG 14.489): on these two formulae, see Korhonen 2004: 88-89. The dedicant has a double name, Λόλλις Εὐτύχης: Λόλλις is not attested elsewhere in Sicily, while Εὐτύχης is found in two Latin inscriptions from the imperial period, CIL 10.7094 = ISic000376 and CIL 10.7099 = ISic000381. The name of his wife, Σαβείνα, is frequent in Sicily and recurs in a Christian inscription from Catania (IG 14.549 = ISic001368).
