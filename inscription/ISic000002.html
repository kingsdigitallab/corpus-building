Apparatus criticus

Line.4: Alessi reported the text as intact and read MELANTHI· ; the text seems to have suffered damaged to the right margin since discovery
Line.4-5: Mommsen suggested 'potius Melanth(e) in suo, quam Melanthin(a) suo'.

English translation

Sacred to the shades of the underworld. Lurius Zosimus lived for 7 years. Luria Melanthin made it for her son.

commentary

The reading Melanthin, based on Alessi's text is acceptable as it is: this is the Latin transliteration of the Greek name Μελάνθιν, a "shortened" diminutive of Μελανθώ (via Μελάνθιον); this type of "shortening" in -ιον and -ιος endings (the exact nature of which, whether phonetic or morphological, is debated) occurs very frequently in inscriptions and papyri of the Roman period. The form Μελάνθιν is attested once in a third-century AD Macedonian inscription (IG 10.2,1 204), whereas LGPN has 4 attestations of Μελανθώ dating to the Hellenistic period.
