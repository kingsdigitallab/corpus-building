Description
A rectangular white marble urn, with a lid. The lid has acroteria on each of the four corners. The urn has square fluted columns depicted in relief on each vertical corner, and a tabula ansata in light relief fills the front face.

The use of the name Flavianus suggests a date after the beginning of the Flavian dynasty (beginning with Vespasian, 69 AD), but Flavianusâ€™ status as slave, freedman, or free remains uncertain. The use of architectural elements in the form of the chest and a tabula ansata to frame the inscription are typical features of ash chests of this period.
