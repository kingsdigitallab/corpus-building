English translation

Stenis Kalinis (son) of Stattis (and) Maras Pompiis (son) of Niumdis, meddices, and the Mamertine people built (this). Sacred to Apollo.

[Latinised] Stenius Calinius (son) of Stattius (and) Maras Pompius (son) of Niumidius, meddices, and the Mamertine people built (this). Sacred to Apollo.

Provenance found
The inscription was originally found in 1612 or 1613 near an old tower in the Giudecca area of Messina, and was recorded by G. Buonfiglio Costanzo. By 1624, when it was recorded by G. Gualtherus, the front left-hand corner of the stone had been cut away. The right-hand edge of the stone was cut away at some later date. The inscription was rediscovered in 1755 and mentioned by Caio Domenico Gallo. It was found again at the beginning of the twentieth century, built into a wall at Via CÃ¡rdines 152, and was moved to the Museo Regionale di Messina in 1909. For more information on the history of the discovery of the inscription, see Crawford (2011) and Crawford ().
