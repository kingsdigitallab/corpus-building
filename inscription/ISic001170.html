Inscription

Layout
Two columns of Greek funerary texts. Right column intact, but left column is almost completely missing. Perhaps a missing epitaph on the right as well. 4 lines of Greek text in each column, text of both columns is roughly in line. The right column has an unusual stepped indentation of the left margin, with each line starting c.1 character further to the right than the last.

commentary

Given the presence of at least two epitaphs, it is likely that this plaque belonged to a family tomb. Herylos is a unique name, not attested elsewhere. Brugnone (1974) suggests that it could be a form of the documented Heryllos, or is a derivative of the Latin Herylus. Herais is an extremely common name in the empire, attested in Sicily only in the masculine on a lead sheet at Kamarina, ISic030053.
