[---το]ῦ ναοῦ βαινέ[τω---]
[---ὁ ληφθ]εὶς ἀδικείσητ[αι ἀζημίως? ---]

Apparatus criticus

Text after Manganaro; Manganaro reads the traces above line 1 as evidence for a preceding line, proposing: [(?)μηδε]ὶς[παρὰ τὸν ὅρον(?)]; Orsi, more plausibly, saw traces of a decorative motif, and considered line 1 the opening line of the text.

translation

commentary

Orsi considered the text to be of Roman period; Manganaro considered Hellenistic, and reconstructs it as a prohibition to enter a sanctuary (seemingly taking the provenance of the Temple of Concord as certain).
