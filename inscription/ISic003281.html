ISic003281. Epitaph for Bassos draft

View in current site

Ἐνθάδε κεῖτε
Βάσσος ἰατρός
ἔζησεν ἔτη
ν´ ((christogram))
5ἐτελεύτησεν
τῇ πρὸ ιε´ ((:)) Κα(λανδῶν) Μαρ(τίων)

Apparatus criticus

Text based on photograph

English translation

Here lies Bassos the doctor. He lived for 50 years, and died 15 days before the Kalends of March.

Italian translation

Qui giace Bassos il medico. Visse 50 anni e morì 15 giorni prima delle Calende di marzo.

commentary

The epitaph presents the traditional expression ἐνθάδε κεῖται (for the phonetic development of κεῖται into κεῖτε from the 3rd cent. AD, see in Catania IG 14.532 = ISic001351, Libertini (1930) 44 no.7 = ISic001629), followed by the name of the deceased, the age and the date of death (in this case expressed with the verb τελευτάω in the aorist form), which is frequent in Christian epitaphs. In this case, there is the mention (rare in Christian epitaphs) of the profession of the deceased, ἰατρός. For other Sicilian inscriptions mentioning doctors, see Rizzo, F. P. (1989), "La menzione del lavoro nelle epigrafi della Sicilia antica: Per una storia della mentalità", pp.62-64 and Manganaro, G. (1994), "Iscrizioni, epigrafi ed epigrammi in Greco della Sicilia orientale di epoca romana", in "Mélanges de l’École Francaise de Rome: Antiquité" 106(1), 79–118, at p.107.
