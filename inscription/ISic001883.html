commentary

The object is published on the basis of a surviving drawing in the archival notes of the archaeologist Giacomo Scibona, but the object itself appears to be lost. It is part of material found in excavation in the area of an extramural sanctuary in the locale of 'Piano Cupa', on the north-west side of anicent Haluntium (San Marco d'Alunzio). The area is said to have been active between the fourth and second century BC, and the object is described from a verbal report as being the lid of a marble pyxis. The lettering is presented in the drawing as very regular and neat, and so may be compatible with this claim, as opposed to being simply a graffito on a ceramic pyxis. The lettering would appear to fit an early hellenistic timeframe.
